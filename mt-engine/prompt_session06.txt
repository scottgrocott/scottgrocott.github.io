Session goal: vehicle terrain physics — cars and forklifts
Current state:

Cars and forklifts exist as enemies with YUKA pathfinding working
They currently slide/glide over terrain with no suspension or physical presence
Physics engine is Rapier (already integrated, trimesh terrain collider in place)
Player has full Rapier rigid body with CCD

Goals:

Terrain-riding suspension — vehicles bounce and tilt on the terrain surface. Implement a simple spring/damper per wheel (4 wheels, raycast down from each corner). The chassis tilts to match terrain slope. No full rigid body vehicle — fake it with raycasts and lerped rotations for performance.
Cliff physics — when a vehicle drives off a cliff (no raycast hit below), apply gravity, let it fall, play out the arc. On landing, apply an impact.
Upside-down detection — if the vehicle's up vector dot world up < 0 (flipped), delete the mesh and remove from enemy registry after a short delay.
Forklift = car driving backwards — forklift uses the same vehicle physics system, just reverses the forward direction so the forks lead.

Key files: cars.js, forklifts.js, enemyBase.js, physics.js
Key constraints:

Vehicles are currently box meshes parented to YUKA entities
getTerrainHeightAt(x, z) is available and fast (pixel lookup, no physics query needed)
Keep YUKA steering — don't replace pathfinding, just layer physics on top of the movement
